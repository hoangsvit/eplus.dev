The **Arcade Facilitator Program** is a no-cost, gamified learning campaign for technical practitioners at every level. Participants can build practical Google Cloud and AI skills, earn shareable credentials, collect Arcade progress, and work toward program milestones.

## Program Timeline

*   **Program starts:** *July 13, 2026 at 5:00 PM GMT+5:30*
    
*   **Program ends:** *September 14, 2026 at 11:59 PM GMT+5:30*
    
*   **Duration:** Two months
    

> The syllabus and active game links may be updated during the cohort. Always check the official page before starting a game.

## Recommended Learning Order

Complete activities in the following order:

1.  **Arcade games first** — they normally have monthly deadlines.
    
2.  **Time-limited special games** — complete them before they disappear.
    
3.  **Skill badge courses** — use the remaining program time to earn as many badges as possible.
    
4.  **Additional catalog badges** — required when targeting the highest milestone.
    

## Arcade Games

Google normally releases **six Arcade games each month**. The monthly lineup can include:

*   Arcade Base Camp
    
*   Arcade Adventure
    
*   Arcade Voyage
    
*   Arcade Trail
    
*   A themed or certification-focused game
    
*   A special Arcade game
    

Across the two-month Facilitator cohort, participants can complete up to **12 monthly game badges**.

For the highest Facilitator milestone, **any 12 eligible game completions** can be counted.

## Reaching the Highest Milestone

The official syllabus currently lists **51 skill badges**.

To reach the highest Facilitator milestone, participants must also find and complete **15 additional eligible skill badges** from the wider Google Skills catalog.

That means the target is:

```text
51 listed skill badges
+ 15 additional catalog skill badges
= 66 skill badge completions
```

The game requirement is:

```text
12 eligible Arcade game completions
```

> Badge availability, course titles, eligibility rules, and milestone calculations may change. Use your Facilitator progress emails and the official syllabus as the final source of truth.

## Important Notes

*   Use the **same Google account** for enrolment and Google Skills.
    
*   Make sure your Google Skills profile is public when the program requires profile validation.
    
*   Starting a lab is not enough; complete the full game or skill badge to earn its credential.
    
*   Do not create duplicate program registrations.
    
*   Complete monthly games before working through badges without deadlines.
    
*   Review progress emails and report incorrect or missing activity as early as possible.
    
*   Avoid relying only on unofficial videos or spreadsheets because game codes and requirements can change.
